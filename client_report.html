Client

I knew right away that I wanted to create something related to cooking. While playing Wordle, I came up with the idea for a "Recipe of the Day" website. I finalized the logo after consulting a friend who specializes in graphic design.

I communicated with the lead developer primarily through email and later via text. The communication went smoothly, and I believe he fully understood my vision for the project.

In fact, the final product exceeded my expectations. Given the complexity of the website, I didn’t anticipate it turning out as well as it did. The lead developer and his team did a fantastic job bringing my ideas to life.

If I were to do a similar project in the future, I would provide more initial content. Since this site was meant to be fairly small, I only gave them ten recipes. Next time, I would aim to include more content to make the site more robust.

Lead Developer

I managed the development team through email and GitHub. Whenever tasks needed to be assigned or issues arose, I used email to communicate our next steps.

I stayed in contact with the client primarily via email. There were a few minor issues, such as delays in receiving the logo and a couple of misunderstandings regarding the website features, but overall the communication was effective.

Publishing and sharing the site was straightforward. GitHub Pages is a very user-friendly platform, and I encountered no significant issues during deployment.

Junior Developer

Before I begin, I want to clarify that I only worked on one of the sites, so my responses will reflect that specific experience.

Our team lead set up a Discord server for communication, which worked well initially. However, when I had specific questions, I didn’t always receive timely responses from the lead developer. Despite that, communication was generally adequate.

Fortunately, the site plan provided enough detail for me to understand my responsibilities and complete my tasks confidently.

I believe I met the client’s expectations. I followed the site plan closely to ensure the design matched the original vision.

I did encounter some issues when trying to publish changes to someone else’s GitHub repository. I asked for help in Discord but didn’t receive a response. Eventually, I received assistance from the instructor, and the publishing process worked fine afterward.

General Experience and Reflections

The most challenging aspect of this project was team communication as a junior developer. Unanswered questions sometimes led to delays and misunderstandings.

My favorite part of the project was definitely designing the site plan. I enjoyed creating the logo and choosing the colors and overall aesthetic for the website.

If I could redo this project as a client, I would include more content from the start. I think the final version felt a bit bare-bones, though that’s not a reflection on the lead developer’s work.

Absolutely. This project gave me valuable insight into what it’s like to work in a team environment. I’ve learned several important lessons about collaboration, communication, and meeting expectations in a real-world setting.
